About Me

Big dreams have always come naturally to me. So how do I get from where I am now to realizing all of my dreams? Hard work. I firmly beleive that you can't get anywhere in life without hard work. Whether it was learning to play an instrument twice my size in elementary school, purchasing and fixing a 50 year old truck with absolutely no experience, or teaching myself HTML in between shifts and class, I work hard to accomplish my goals. My friends might tell you that I'm eccentric and can be unrealistic at times. I would disagree with that. Something is unrealistic only if you are unwilling to work towards it. My outlook on life is this, anything is possible if you're passionate, dedicate the necessary time, and work until the only possible outcome is success.

I want to work for a company that makes a difference. I want to work at a company that impacts the lives of both its employees and customers. What's the point of working if you aren't making a difference? In my opinion, there is none. So who do I want to work for? There are undoubtedly a countless number that are making a difference, so it would be foolish to focus on just one or two well-known companies. I want to keep my mind, and options open, but trust me when I say I'll know when I find the perfect one.

In my free time I play the upright Bass, most recently in the BYU University String Orchestra and the BYU Jazz Lab Ensemble. I have played soccer since I was young, and since moving to BYU I've been playing intramural soccer on and off. I also love learning about and working on old cars. My last project and baby was a 1964 Ford F-250 that I bought from my grandfather, but currently my father and I are working on his baby - a 1957 Chevrolet Bel Air. I've always been fascinated with technology which has led me to build my own desktop computer, participate in the Google Glass Explorer Program, participate in hackathons, and spend countless hours messing around with various programming languages in the hopes of becoming a real-life "programmer."
